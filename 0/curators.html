Two Major Acquisitions Announced

Two curators of extraordinary distinction will join the museum's curatorial staff:
  • Charles Stuckey, Ph.D. has been appointed Patrick and Aimee Butler Curator. He will have responsibility for European and American paintings and for 19th- and 20th- century sculpture. Charles Stuckey comes to the MIA from the Chicago Art Institute (AIC), where since 1987 he has served as the Frances and Thomas Dittmer Curator of Twentieth-Century Painting and Sculpture. Among his achievements are retrospective exhibitions of works by Toulouse-Lautrec, Berthe Morisot, Gaugin and, of course, Claude Monet. The culmination of Stuckey's life-long interest and unparalleled expertise in the area of Impressionism, the Monet exhibition reigns as the celebrated star of current exhibitions. Stuckey's accomplishments at the AIC also included such highly acclaimed projects as the renovation and reinstallation of the 20th century art collection.










  • Christopher Monkhouse is the new Bell Curator with responsibilities for decorative arts, sculpture, and architecture. Monkhouse is currently the Curator of Architecture, Heinz Architectural Center, The Carnegie Museum of Art, Pittsburg, Pennsylvania, where he organized the soon-to-open exhibition, "Monolithic Architecture." The founding curator of European and American Decorative Art at the Museum of Art, Rhode Island School of Design (RISD) in Providence (1976-1991). During the 15 years at RISD he organized over 50 exhibitions and restored, to its 1906 appearance, Pendleton House - the first "American Wing" in a United States art museum. In 1986 he co-authored a catalogue of the distinguished, RISD collection of American Furniture.
