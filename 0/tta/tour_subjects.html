Tour Subjects

  1. African Art
    Explore the rich diversity of masks, textiles, and other objects from western and central Africa.
  2. American Art
    This tour explores art produced in America from colonial times to the present by Euro-American and Native American artists.
  3. Art of the Americas
    This newly reinstalled gallery exhibits the art and culture of the indigenous peoples of North, Meso-, and South America.
  4. Asian Art
    This survey of the museum's collection of Asian art includes the world-famous Chinese bronzes and jades and Japanese sculptures, screens, and paintings.
  5. City and Country
    Examine landscapes and city scenes to observe how artists relate to their environment.
  6. Classics and the Classical Influence
    Explore the art of ancient Greece and Rome and trace the influence of their art and architecture through the centuries.
  7. Decorative Arts and the Period Rooms
    Explore the museum's period rooms and its collection of furniture, silver, ceramics, and glass, and textiles.
  8. European Art
    This survey of art from the Middle Ages through the early 18th century includes works by Rembrandt, El Greco, and Nicolas Poussin.
  9. Highlights of the Museum's Collection
    Enjoy a cross-cultural sampling of our best-known works of art from many different time periods and parts of the world.
  10. How Was It Made?
    Examine how the materials, methods, and circumstances of production affect finished works of art.
  11. Nineteenth and Twentieth-Century Art
    Follow the development of modern art through selected works from the 19th century to the present time.
  12. People and Places
    Portraits and landscapes show how people lived in many time periods and geographic areas.
  13. Visual Elements
    Examine the artist's choices of color, line, and shape in selected works of art.
  14. Women and Art
    Explore works of art created by women and examine the manner in which women have been depicted through the ages in art.
