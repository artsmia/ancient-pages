Milestones of Modernism
1880-1940

Selections from the Norwest Collection

Last year, Norwest Bank generously donated its acclaimed Modernism collection to The Minneapolis Institute of Arts. It constitutes one of the largest and most significant gifts in the museum's distinguished history.

The term Modernism commonly applies to those forward-looking architects, designers and artisans who, from the 1880s on, forged a new and diverse vocabulary principally to escape Historicism, the tyranny of previous historical styles. Comprising nearly 500 works, the collection traces the accelerated progression of decorative, applied and graphic design over a sixty year period, from 1880 to 1940. It was the first and only corporate collection devoted to modernist design.

This exhibition features a selection of works in various media drawn from the entire collection. For the past decade, these works have been exhibited in Norwest Center by means of rotating exhibitions, each examining aspects of modernist design. This premiere showing provides a comprehensive overview documenting the movements (milestones) represented in this remarkable collection:

• British Arts and Crafts (1880-1910)

• American Arts and Crafts (1900-1915)

• European Art Nouveau (1880-1905)

• American Art Nouveau (1890-1910)

• Wiener Werkstatte (1903-1933)

• De Stijl (1917-1928)

• Bauhaus (1919-1933)

• European Art Deco (1920-1940)

• American Art Deco (1920-1940)

In the spring of 2000, a selection of works will be on view and rotated in a new gallery devoted to modernist design where objects from the Norwest collection will be displayed with related examples already in the Institute's collection.
