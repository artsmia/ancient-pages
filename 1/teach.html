Summer Institute for Teachers

The Art of Looking
Monday, June 18 to Friday, June 29, 2001
Weekdays, 9:30 a.m.–4 p.m.

Explore strategies for looking at art, in the company of fellow teachers from many disciplines. Develop lesson plans to take back to your classroom and select visual resources to use with your lessons. Graduate credit is available through Hamline University for an additional $310.

Fee: $100 members, $125 nonmembers
Registration and payment due by June 1
To register, call (612) 870-3013

Thursday, February 22
Making the Most of a Self-Guided Visit
5–8 p.m., Classroom 112

Learn how to make your self-guided group visit fun and productive. Includes a box dinner, a teacher's packet and a set of 20 postcards.

Thursday, March 29
Materials and Techniques of the Greco-Roman Artist
5–8 p.m., Classroom 112

Dig in to the Classical Gallery with an archeologist's eye. Caroline Nicholson, contributing editor to Dig, an archeology magazine for kids, will join us. Includes a box dinner and a teacher's packet with 5 slides.

Registration and payment due by March 23.

Thursday, April 26
A Tibetan View of the Cosmos
5–8 p.m., Classroom 112

Explore Tibet and its traditions of sacred art, including a special selection of works on display in honor of the Dalai Lama's visit to the Twin Cities in May. Includes a box dinner and a teacher's packet with 5 slides.

Registration and payment due by April 20.
