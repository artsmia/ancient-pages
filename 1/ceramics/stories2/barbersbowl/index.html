Mayan Story:

This Mayan creation story is based on several traditional creation myths. The stories have been adapted here by a contemporary historian of Mayan culture, Carlos Abler. The voice is Carlos Abler's.
(Story length is 7 minutes 47 seconds)

Hopi Story:

This Hopi creation story, originally passed down for generations through oral tradition, was taken from, Pueblos Story Teller, by Diane Hoyt-Goldsmith. Like many traditional stories, this one plays an important role in the lives of the Hopi people. The Hopi are a Pueblo group that live in the Southwest part of the United States. The voice is that of Carley Bordeau.
(Story length is 4 minutes 16 seconds)

Ife Story:

This story is a fictionalized account of the experience of a young boy, and gives some idea of how a child may have felt acquiring the powerful position of Oni (a king descended from a god). Written by Jennifer Lack, the story incorporates the creation myth that established Ife as the sacred city of the Yoruba people of West Africa. Read by Amaka Omuso.
(Story length is 5 minutes 7 seconds)

Swedish Jar:

This fictional story is set at the turn of the century in Stockholm, Sweden where, art, industrialism and the role of women in society were changing dramatically. Written by Jennifer Lack, the story is about how two young girls' lives are influenced by such changes. The voice is Solveig Beckmen's.
(Story length is 5 minutes)

Earth Spirits:

This story set during the Tang Dynasty in China incorporates details recorded in Chinese writings of the time. In A.D. 742 the Emperor set limits on the size of funerals and number of Earth Spirits allowed for each, as wealthy families were wiped out by the expenses. Written by Jennifer Lack, the story is a about a curious young boy as he watches a funeral procession with his father. The voice is that of Eugene Kim Goldmund.
(Story length is 3 minutes 14 seconds)

Hungarian Zsolnay Vase:

Set in the 1920's, this story is adapted from the Hungarian book, "Zsolnay", in which Terez Zsolnay Mattyasovszky wrote about her childhood. Terez worked in her family's ceramic factory during the years that her brother ran it. Written by Jennifer Lack, the story is about Terez, as a grandmother, as she sits with her granddaughter and reads a diary entry she wrote during her childhood. The diary entry is fictitious, but uses some descriptions and incidents from Terez's writings. Read by Katherine Lack.
(Story length is 6 minutes 51 seconds)
