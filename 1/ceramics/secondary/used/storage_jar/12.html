The people must have buried the jars in the ground inside their homes. You know, they didn't have wood floors or carpeting then. And this way they could put things inside the jars and get at them easily.

The jars would have been sort of like kitchen cupboards.

Putting a jar in the ground gave it support and made it less likely to break.

But the top of the jar, the part that didn't get buried, was decorative and looked nice.
