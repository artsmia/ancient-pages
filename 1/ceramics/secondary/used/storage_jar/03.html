Did you know that Japan wasn't always made up of islands?

Thousands of years ago, many people had to MIGRATE from the lowlands to the highlands of what is now the country of Japan.

Why? you ask.

Well, the earth went through a time of global warming, with higher-than-usual temperatures. Glaciers began to melt, and lowlands were flooded by the melting ice.

The rising water separated Japan from the mainland, creating the islands that exist today.
