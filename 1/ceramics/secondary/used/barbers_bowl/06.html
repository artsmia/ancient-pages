As a person who looks at objects from the past to learn about the past, I have learned that because the barber had many duties, the barber's bowl had many FUNCTIONS.

In the late 1600s, around the time that English colonists were coming to America, the barber was known as a barber-surgeon.

He did not simply cut hair; he also performed medical services, like BLOODLETTING.

The red stripe on the barber pole stands for blood and the white stripe for the bandage.
