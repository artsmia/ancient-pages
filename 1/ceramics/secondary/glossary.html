ABSTRACT: Images in which natural things have been changed in order to focus the eye on color and FORM; the result does not look realistic.

ABSTRACTED: Images that have been SIMPLIFIED, EXAGGERATED or otherwise changed to not look realistic and to focus the eye on color and FORM.

ADVERTISE: The promotion of an idea or product with the intention of convincing people to buy the product or support the idea.

AESTHETIC: A CULTURE's idea of what art and beauty are; discussion related to issues of beauty.

AFTERLIFE: Life after death; some CULTURES believe there is a life after death.

AGRARIAN SOCIETY: A community or CULTURE that lives off the land as farmers.

APPLIED: Added on; as in "applied decoration."

APPRENTICESHIP: Learning a job by training with an expert who is already doing that job.

ARCHEOLOGIST: Someone who studies the life and customs of past cultures by looking at things they left behind (examples: utensils, pottery, architecture, etc.)

ARID: A climate that is hot and dry, where it is difficult to grow food crops.

ART NOUVEAU: An art STYLE from the late 1800s, especially used in architecture and interior design, that can be IDENTIFIED by its curved lines and STYLIZED IMAGES of plants.

ARTIFACTS: Objects, like pottery, that were left behind by people from the past.

ASSEMBLED: Put together.

ASYMMETRY: An arrangement of FORMS and SHAPES that does not appear to be the same on either side of an imaginary center line.

ATTRIBUTES: Particular CHARACTERISTICS, objects, or qualities used to identify something.

BALANCE: The way elements or pieces of an artwork are arranged to create a feeling of stability.

BIOMORPHIC: A form or shape that is irregular and looks like a living organism; the opposite of geometric, which is regular.

BLOOD-LETTING: The practice of curing illnesses by draining "bad blood" out of the body. This is not practiced today.

BODY LANGUAGE: The way movements of the body can communicate a message; for example, some people cross their arms if they are mad.

BORROWED: An idea taken from someone else and changed in a way that makes it your own; an "artistic influence."

CALLIGRAPHY: The art of writing.

CARICATURE: An image that captures the likeness of the person, but does not look REALISTIC because features are EXAGGERATED.

CERAMIC: An object made of clay and then FIRED in a KILN.

CERAMIC FIGURE: A clay object that has been FIRED in a KILN that is in the form of a body, like the human figure or an animal figure.

CERAMIC VESSEL: A clay object that has been FIRED in a KILN. This object can be used to hold or store things like water, grain, or food.

CEREMONIAL: Used in a ritual or a formal event.

CHAMBER: An enclosed space or room, as in a TOMB.

CHARACTERISTIC: A feature that identifies something; for example, brown eyes are a characteristic of some people.

CHEMIST: A scientist who learns about what a substance is made of and why some substances interact and react the way they do.

CIRCULAR: Round in SHAPE.

COBALT BLUE: A greenish blue color in a ceramic GLAZE.

COILING METHOD: A way to make a ceramic vessel out of pieces of clay that have been rolled to look like snakes.

COMMEMORATION: Helping the living remember a person who has died through a memorial; for example, a statue in the park or a special issue postage stamp.

COMPETITION: When at least two people challenge one another to be the best at something.

CONCAVE: A SHAPE that is rounded inward like a bowl.

CONE: A THREE-DIMENSIONAL triangle, like a sugar ice cream cone.

CONFUCIANISM: A system of beliefs developed by the philosopher Confucius, that influenced the traditional CULTURE of China. Confucianism places high value on learning and stresses family relationships.

CONVEX: A SHAPE that is curved or rounded like the outside of a ball.

CULTURE: A group of people who share similar beliefs and values.

DAOISM: A religion native to China. Those who live this religion live according to the Dao or the "Way," which they believe governs the universe.

DECOMPOSE: When something breaks down into smaller parts or another material altogether.

DECORATE, DECORATED: To add something to an artwork in order to make it more pleasing to look at.

DECORATION: Something added to an artwork that makes it pleasing to look at.

DELFTWARE: A type of ceramic pottery (TIN-GLAZED EARTHENWARE) that originated in the down of Delft, Holland.

DESIGN: The planning and decisions that go into making something. Or, a DECORATION.

DESIGNED: Something that has been purposefully planned.

DESIGNER: A person who plans how a product should look.

DETAIL: A specific characteristic, often small but important in a larger context; for example, the particular color of a "scrunchy" may tie together all the parts of an outfit.

DRAPED: Fabric that is placed or laid over something.

EARTHENWARE: POROUS clay that has been FIRED in a KILN at low heat.

ENVIRONMENT: The world around you; for example, if you are outside, your environment may include grass, trees, flowers, birds, etc.

EXAGGERATED: Something made bigger or more prominent than it normally is.

FACIAL FEATURES: The parts of the face: eyes, ears, nose, mouth, chin and cheeks.

FEATURES: A person's unique appearance; how specific parts of a person look; usually referring to a person's face.

FIGURE: Bodily SHAPE or FORM of a living thing.

FIGURINE: A small carved or MOLDED FORM or SHAPE of something, STATUETTE.

FIRED, FIRING: The process of baking clay to take all the moisture out of it and make it very hard. This is done in a fire or in an oven called a KILN.

FORM: A THREE-DIMENSIONAL SHAPE; for example, a SPHERE, a cube, a CONE, or a BIOMORPHIC form.

FLATTENED SPHERE: A SPHERE that has been smashed so that it is no longer round, like a ball.

FUNCTION: The action or purpose of something; for example, a VESSEL's function is to hold or store something, like water or food.

GEOMETRIC SHAPE: A SHAPE with a regular contour, such as a square, triangle, or circle.

GLAZE: In ceramics, a thin coating of minerals that coats the clay. The GLAZE is fixed by baking the clay in a KILN or oven, which makes the surface smooth, shiny (glasslike), and waterproof.

GREEK MYTHOLOGY: Beliefs held by the ancient Greeks that explained why things were the way they were. Greek Mythology included gods and goddesses in human form, who had superhuman powers.

GROUND: The background, or surface to be decorated.

HALF-SPHERE: A THREE-DIMENSIONAL FORM; one half of a ball.

HAND-MADE: Something that is made by human hands, not by machines, and therefore not identical to any other version of it.

HARMONY: The repetition and blending of LINE and color that creates BALANCE and makes an artwork pleasing to look at.

HEADDRESS: An elaborate covering for the head, frequently denoting role or status in a culture.

HEXAGON: A SHAPE that has six sides.

HEXAGRAM: A grouping of two TRIGRAMS.

HIEROGLYPHICS: A system of writing created by the ancient Egyptians that used pictorial characters.

HOPI-TEWA: The Native American people who live in northeastern Arizona; formed by the joining of two separate peoples, the Hopi and the Tewa.

HORIZONTAL: Something, like a line, that goes from left to right, right to left, or is wider than it is tall.

IDEALISM: Believing that things can be perfect.

IDEALIZATION: To change something from the real world that is not perfect into something that is better, more like the idea of perfection.

IDEALIZED: Created according to a standard (an idea) of beauty, perfection, or excellence.

IDENTIFIED: Recognized as something or someone in particular.

IFE: A Yoruba CULTURE in what today is Nigeria, Africa.

IMAGE: A picture or impression of a person, thing or idea.

IMAGINATIVE: Creative; not necessarily REALISTIC.

INCISED: Scratched into the surface of the clay while it is still damp; incised DECORATION.

INDUSTRIAL REVOLUTION: A rapid major change in an economy and/or CULTURE, usually due to the introduction of power-driven machinery and wage labor.

INDUSTRY: A craft, art or business that employs a lot of people and is profitable.

INKSTONE: A container that holds water, made of something rough enough so it will grind the Chinese scholar's ink stick. Slate is the most common, but unglazed clay works, too.

INSPIRED: Motivated to do something.

IRIDESCENT: Displaying a LUSTROUS rainbowlike array of colors that change depending on the angle of light.

KICK WHEEL: See POTTER'S WHEEL.

KILN: An oven-like structure that bakes clay and turns it into the hard durable substances we call CERAMICS.

LINES: The most familiar of all the elements of art; lines can be spiral, curved, straight, zigzag or dotted.

LOIN-CLOTH: A short length of cloth worn around the waist in warm climates.

LUSTER GLAZE, LUSTROUS: A GLAZE containing silver of copper oxide that, when FIRED, prouces an IRIDESCENT surface.

LUXURY: Something that is not necessary, but makes life more comfortable; for example, a T.V.

MAGYARS: The original group of people who lived in Hungary.

MEDIEVAL: Taking place in or CHARACTERISTIC of the Middle Ages. The "Middle Ages" refers to a time of European history that began around 600 A.D. and continued until about 1500 A.D.

MESA: A piece of land that is raised up above a plateau. It looks like a very large hill with a flat top and steep sides. Mesa means "table" in Spanish.

MOLD: A device used to reproduce identical CERAMIC objects; the mold contains a negative impression of the positive FORM of the object being made.

MODELED: A work of art, usually of clay or wax, that has been shaped by hand or with tools.

MORAL: Belief that determines, for an individual or CULTURE, what is right and what is wrong.

MOTIF: A theme, IMAGE, or PATTERN in a work of art.

MYTH: A belief by a group of people that explains to them why something is the way it is.

NAMPEYO: The name of a distinguished HOPI-TEWA family of potters.

NATIONALISTS: Those who show loyalty and devotion to a nation, placing an emphasis on promoting its CULTURE and interests, and sharing with others the desire for independence from outside influences.

NOBILITY: A group of people who, because of their family relations, had high social status.

OBSERVANT: Pays close attention to something; someone who looks very carefully.

ORGANIC SHAPE: One made with curving or irregular lines; for example, the flowing, twisting SHAPE of a river or the jagged SHAPE of rocky bluffs.

OXIDATION: A FIRING process in which extra oxygen is present in the KILN to change the chemical composition of the glazes on clay objects while they are being FIRED.

PATTERN: Regularly repeated SHAPES, LINES, and colors.

PERSONAL EXPRESSION: Revealing something about yourself (your values, your concerns, your fears, etc.), through your art.

PHILOSOPHY: An overall vision of or attitude about life and the purpose of life.

PIGMENT: Another word for color; a variety of minerals and natural substances can be used as pigments.

PORCELAIN: A fine-grained, non-POROUS CERAMIC ware that when FIRED at high temperatures creates a white, translucent, and extremely hard clay body. Also called hard-paste or "true" porcelain. First produced in China about 900 A.D. during the T'ang Dynasty.

POROUS: A clay that, after firing, has enough tiny holes in it that the vessel will not hold water.

PORTRAIT: A SCULPTURE that represents a person, living or dead, especially in the details of their face.

POSE: A position you put your body into.

POSTER: An image, usually including words, that is attached to walls to communicate information. Before T.V. or radio, the poster was a way for businesses to ADVERTISE their products or services.

POTTER'S WHEEL: A flat HORIZONTAL disk that revolves at the top of a centered stem or axis. Potters sit at the wheel and it is used to shape clay into circular FORMS.

POTSHERD: A fragment of a broken ceramic vessel or object.

PRINTS: Reproduced or duplicated images. For example, in Japanese woodblock prints, an image is carved into the surface of a piece of wood. Ink is rolled across the surface and a piece of paper is placed over the block, then rubbed so that the paper makes contact with all the raised, inked areas of the block. More than one image can be made from the same block.

PROPORTION: How the size of one SHAPE or FORM relates to another.

PUEBLO: The name we use to refer to a Native American group of people who live in Arizona and New Mexico; the word means "village" and refers to the clustered, flat-roofed adobe or stone houses in which these people live.

PUKI: The shallow bowl that some Native American potters use as a base in which to build up a coil-method pot; the round bottom of the puki makes it easy to turn the pot and apply even pressure when smoothing out the coils.

REALISM: A STYLE of art in which the subject is portrayed as closely as possible to the way the human eye sees it.

REALISTIC, REALISTICALLY: True to nature or real life.

REPEAT: To do the same thing over and over.

REPRODUCE: To make an exact copy of something.

RIM: The top edge of a CERAMIC VESSEL.

RITUAL: Formal repetition of certain acts that are part of a ceremony.

SCARIFICATION: The process of making scratches or small cuts in the skin to form decorative marks or scars. Used to mark the status or identity of a person.

SCHOLAR: A person who has done advanced study in a special field, who does original research, and contributes to the body of knowledge in his or her field.

SCULPTURE: A three-dimensional artwork.

SEMI-CIRCLE: Half of a circle.

SGRAFFITO: A type of CERAMIC DECORATION where DESIGNS are produced by scratching or cutting away parts of a top layer (such as GLAZE or SLIP) to form a PATTERN and reveal the contrasting color or texture underneath.

SHAFT-CHAMBER TOMBs: An underground burial place that is accessed through a narrow passage from above, as in the Nayarit culture of Mexico.

SHAPE: The outline of something. Shapes are classified as GEOMETRIC (squares, circles, triangles) and ORGANIC (free, natural, irregular).

SILICA: Silicon dioxide (SiO2) which exists as sand in its impure form.

SIMPLIFYING: Taking away DETAILS.

SLAB: A piece of clay that is flat and thin, like a pancake.

SLIP: In ceramics, a thin mixture of clay and water.

SOCIAL EQUALITY: When all people are treated as equals.

SPHERE: A perfectly round ball, like a basketball or baseball.

SPIRIT: Your most essential inner self; in some cultures, also your relationship to the universe.

SPIRITUALITY: Your belief in how everything relates in the world. For some people, spirituality takes the form of their religious values.

STATUETTE: A small clay FIGURE.

STILL-LIFE: An IMAGE of a group of objects arranged by an artist to be painted or photographed.

STYLE: The characteristic look that works of art from a particular artist, group, CULTURE, movement, or historic time period have in common.

STYLIZED: DESIGNED by SIMPLIFYING or consistently changing SHAPEs or FORMS, often according to a PATTERN.

SURFACE: In ceramics, usually the outside of a vessel or sculpture that is glazed, painted, or otherwise decorated.

SYMBOL, SYMBOLIC: A thing or person that stands for another thing or idea. In some CULTURES a heart is a symbol (is symbolic) of love.

SYMMETRY/SYMMETRICAL: Balance achieved in a work of art by distributing any element (lines, figures, colors, etc.) equally on both sides of an imaginary center line.

TERRA-COTTA: Literally, "cooked earth." Terra-cotta is raw clay that has ground up bits of hard, fired clay mixed in; it is extremely durable after firing.

THREE-DIMENSIONAL: Something that has or looks like it has height, width and depth (volume). Example: A ball of clay or a basketball.

THROWING: Forming a clay vessel using a POTTER'S WHEEL.

TIN-GLAZED EARTHENWARE: Lightly FIRED pottery, usually buff or pink in color, that is coated on the SURFACE with an OPAQUE GLAZE. The GLAZE contains fine sand, known as SILICA, calcined lead, and tin oxides. The tin oxides, or ash, make the glaze opaque. Common types of tin-glazed EARTHENWARE are known as MAJOLICA, Faience, and DELFTWARE.

TOMB: An underground chamber where a dead person is buried.

TORSO: The part of the human body that is between the head and the legs.

TRADE ROUTE: A well-traveled way that people would follow to trade goods with others, usually avoiding the worst terrain.

TRADEMARK: A SYMBOL used to identify a specific business or product.

TRANSLUCENT: Material that transmits light but is not TRANSPARENT, such as true PORCELAIN.

TRANSPARENT: Material through which you can see in great detail, such as glass.

TRIGRAMS: Any of eight combinations of three whole or broken lines grouped together to form characters. Trigrams are used by the Chinese in pairs called HEXAGRAMS to foretell future events or discover hidden knowledge.

UTILITARIAN: Relating to something that is useful or was DESIGNED for a specific purpose.

VERTICAL LINES: LINES that go up and down, top to bottom.

VESSEL: Something that can hold things within, such as water or food.

YORUBA: People of southwestern Nigeria, who are one of the largest art- producing groups in Africa. Their ruling family goes back to the 9th century A.D.

YUCCA: A plant with a long, narrow, fibrous leaf, woody stem, and bell-shaped drooping flowers found in an area ranging from the Southwestern United States to Central America. Native American potters use the leaves to make paintbrushes.
