Now, let¹s think about something here...

The ceramic vessel you are looking at is called a "seed jar."

Jars like this were used in the past for storing seeds for the next year's planting.

But...

I also said that the mesas where the Hopi live are arid lands.

What do plants need really badly to be able to grow and be healthy?

WATER. Which is scarce in an arid climate. This is why the Nampeyo family uses feather and wing motifs on their pottery pieces.

Birds, especially eagles, play an important ceremonial role in the life of the Hopi people. The Hopi believe the spirit of the eagle carries a prayer for rain to the gods on behalf of the people.
