Today, the details of life in Ife are lost to us, and we can only guess what this SCULPTURE meant to the people living at the time it was made.

As an art historian, I look at objects from Ife's CULTURE to try and learn what life was like there.

This sculpture is called a shrine head. In many West African cultures, it is common for sculptures representing dead ancestors to be grouped on an altar in a holy place of devotion called a shrine.
