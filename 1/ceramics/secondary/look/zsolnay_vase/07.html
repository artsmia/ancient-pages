The Zsolnay vases has a story too.

During the time this vase was made part of the country was ruled by Germany, but the MAGYARS (the native Hungarian people) and many other NATIONALIST groups wanted to be left alone.

The images on the vases combined traditional Hungarian DECORATION with the art style of ART NOUVEAU, or "new art."

In Hungary, this style was called Jugendstil, a German word meaning "young style."

Since the Germans ruled Hungary at the time, the Hungarians used the German word.
