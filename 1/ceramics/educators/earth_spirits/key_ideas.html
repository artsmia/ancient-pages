Chinese (Tang dynasty)
Tomb Retinue: Earth Spirits
About A.D. 725
Glazed white clay
37-1/2 inches high
The Ethel Morrison Van Derlip Fund
49.1.9,10

  • These earth spirits display the vitality, cosmopolitanism, and technical advances that characterized the Tang (tahng) DYNASTY in China.

     
  • Like most Tang pottery figures, these large earth spirits were made by pressing clay into molds.

     
  • The rare and costly cobalt blue GLAZE found on these figures was usually reserved for the best and most striking figures.

     
  • These two intimidating winged creatures were buried in a tomb to guard and protect the dead in the afterlife.
