The Subject

Sarah Allen, a resident of Gloucester, Massachusetts, was 34 years old when John Singleton Copley painted her portrait. The artist also painted a portrait of her husband, Nathaniel Allen, a successful businessperson and active politician. The Allens were among the less than 1% of colonial America's population who had portraits painted. Since only wealthy colonists could afford portraits, these paintings were status symbols.
