Portrait of Abigail Gowen
About 1763
Oil on canvas
20 1/4 x 16 1/8 inches
Gift of Rhodes Robertson 70.33.5


Title
Art in the Colonies ~ About the Artist ~ About this Object ~ Look Questions ~ Think Questions
Key Points

  • The earliest portraitists in the colonies were self-taught and often had to combine portrait painting with other trades to make a living.
  • By the 18th century, colonists began to COMMISSION portraits to decorate their homes and to give to relatives.
  • In spite of this portrait's incorrect proportions, details such as the blushing cheeks and small lips suggest that the artist, Joseph Badger, captured something of Abigail Gowen's appearance.
