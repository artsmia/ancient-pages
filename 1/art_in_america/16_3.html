Development of Minneapolis

Around 1902, when Koehler painted Rainy Evening on Hennepin Avenue, Minneapolis was undergoing tremendous growth. Conditions of life were changing dramatically because of increasing population, advancing technology and industry, and the shift from a rural to an urban society. Many of the buildings in this scene were constructed during the building boom of the 1880s, when Minneapolis prospered from the commercial development of lumbering, flour milling, and trade.
