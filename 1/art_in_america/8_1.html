Key Points

  • Hiram Powers based this NEOCLASSICAL marble PORTRAIT of George Washington on ancient Roman portrait BUSTS.
  • Powers, like most American sculptors in the 19th century, spent most of his career in Italy, the home of great sculptural traditions since ancient Roman times.
  • Although Washington's face is very NATURALISTIC, the toga he wears presents him in the IDEALIZED guise of a Roman senator.
