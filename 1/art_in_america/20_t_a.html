Think Questions Teacher Answer Key

1. What do you think these people wanted to convey about themselves in this PORTRAIT? What aspects of the photo led you to your answer?

2. Why might James van DerZee have chosen to pose this couple and their car in front of these buildings? (Because the buildings are visually interesting; they characterize the urban environment; perhaps the couple lived there, etc.) How does the repetition of forms in the buildings contribute to the visual interest of this photograph? Why might van DerZee want to contrast the new car with the old buildings?

3. What do you consider the most important aspect of this photograph? Why?

4. Have you ever had your PORTRAIT taken by a professional photographer? Who made decisions about what you would wear? Where the photo would be taken? How you would pose? What props would be included? James van DerZee thought that his subjects should decide these things for themselves. If you could make such decisions, what would you want your photograph to look like?

5. Discuss the Harlem Renaissance. What evidence in this photograph tells you that Harlem was a thriving community at this time?

6. Compare and contrast James van DerZee's photograph Harlem Couple with Romare Bearden's GOUACHE Factory Workers. What do they have in common? (Both show people; both show aspects of urban life.) What things does each artist use to tell us about the people? (Buildings, surroundings, dress, poses.) Van DerZee's photograph is a PORTRAIT. Would you consider Bearden's gouache a portrait also? Why or why not?
