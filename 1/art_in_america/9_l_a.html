Look Questions Teacher Answer Key

1. Look closely at this LANDSCAPE painting. What do you see? (Waterfalls, storm clouds, trees, an American Indian, rocks, etc.)

2. What colors did the artist Henry Lewis use most in this painting? (Greens, browns, blues, and whites.) Where do you see pink in the painting? (In the clouds and a touch reflected in the water.) Where do you see red? (In the small detail of the American Indian.)

3. If you could walk into this LANDSCAPE painting, where would you enter? What makes this point the most inviting place to enter? Once you entered the landscape, what sounds might you hear? What scents might you smell?

4. ATMOSPHERIC PERSPECTIVE is a technique used by artists to show distance. With this technique, objects that are farther away appear lighter and less detailed than objects that are closer. Where do you see atmospheric perspective in this painting? (The trees and land behind the falls appear smaller, lighter, and less clear than those closer .)

5. Lewis sketched this scene as he floated down the river on a pontoon. Where do you see moving water in this painting? (Water crashes against rocks, giving the appearance that it is moving quickly.) Where else does Lewis show movement? (Storm clouds passing; some suggestion of movement in wispy white clouds.)

6. What do you think the weather is like, based on the sky? (Cloudy, but clearing after a storm has just passed.) Where is the sun? (Not visible, but pink hue on clouds suggests it is just rising or setting.) Is it warm or cold? How can you tell? (The cloudy sky; the American Indian is wrapped in a blanket.)
