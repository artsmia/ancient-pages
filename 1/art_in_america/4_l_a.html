Look Questions Teacher Answer Key

1. What type of painting is this? (PORTRAIT.) Who is the subject? (A woman; Mrs. Allen.) Describe Mrs. Allen: What color is her hair? Is her nose large or small? Are her lips thin or full? Is her chin long or short? Is this a NATURALISTIC portrait? Why or why not?

2. What is Mrs. Allen wearing? (Blue satin dress, white gloves, hat.) What is she doing? (Posing; she is standing, slightly turned towards the viewer; she appears to be removing her glove.) Where is she? (Outdoors.) What is in the BACKGROUND? (Trees, sky.)

3. What colors did this artist use the most? (Blue, green, gray.) Are they COOL or WARM COLORS? (Cool.) What other colors do you see? (Flesh, pink, golden.) Are these cool or warm colors? (Warm.) Where does the artist use black and white to show shadows and light reflections?

4. The artist John Singleton Copley used oil paint on canvas to create the illusion of a variety of TEXTURES. Which parts of this painting appear to be silky or smooth? Which parts appear rough? Name various parts of Mrs. Allen's clothing and describe how they might feel if you could touch them.

5. Without knowing anything about Mrs. Allen, what can you tell (or guess) about her from this PORTRAIT? About how old is she? (Thirty-four.) Why do you think so? Is she rich or poor? (Rich.) How can you tell? (She wears fine clothes and she can afford to have a portrait made.) What kind of work do you think she did? (Can't tell from this portrait.) What kind of activities might she do in this dress? (Leisure activities, entertaining company, posing for a picture.)
