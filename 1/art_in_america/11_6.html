Technique

A woman made this jar using traditional techniques. She formed the base and then built the walls by pinching together successive coils of clay. The potter then scraped the outside to remove excess clay and achieve a uniform thickness and smoothness. Using the chewed end of a yucca leaf as a brush, she covered the dried jar with a thin mixture of white clay. Finally, she painted the black and red patterns with mineral and vegetable pigments. To harden the pot, she fired it over a flame fueled by manure.
