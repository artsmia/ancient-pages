The Tradition of Feasting

In 1621 the Pilgrims celebrated their first harvest in the "New World" by enjoying a feast with the Native Americans who had helped them survive that first year. This was the beginning of an American tradition known today as the national holiday of Thanksgiving. The Woodlands Indians on the eastern seaboard, however, had celebrated a form of thanksgiving feast for centuries. For them, the celebration with the Pilgrims was part of an ancient tradition of offering thanks to the Creator for the abundance of the earth.
