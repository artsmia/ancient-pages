Nina Simone

Eunice Waymon, who later changed her name to Nina Simone, was born in 1933 in North Carolina. She gained national prominence as a jazz pianist and singer during the late 1950s. Her unique form of jazz often incorporated elements of folk, gospel, African, and contemporary music. After having worked in Europe for several decades, Simone has recently made a comeback in the United States.
