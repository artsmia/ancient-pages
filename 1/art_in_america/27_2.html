Chuck Close

After beginning his career as an ABSTRACT painter, Chuck Close decided to limit his subjects to information gathered from photographs. He also determined that he would make large-scale works because he wanted his work to be noticed. During the late 1960s, he began painting huge black-and-white portraits of himself and friends. His early paintings are now associated with a group of artists called photorealists, who used photographs as the sources for their paintings. Like other art of his era, Close's paintings are in large part about the making of art.
