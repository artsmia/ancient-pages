Look Questions Teacher Answer Key

1. What is Frank wearing? (T-shirt, work shirt, glasses.) What do his clothes tell you about him? Where is Frank? (In a city.) How can you tell? (The reflection in his glasses shows city buildings.)

2. Is this a photograph or a painting? (A painting.) How can you tell? What makes it look like a photograph? (It is black and white; some parts look out of focus; it looks so real.)

3. Where do you see the sharpest detail in this painting? (Front part of his hair, glasses, chin hairs.) Where do you see the least amount of detail? (Ears and hair behind ears.) Why do you think these areas look less detailed? (They are out of focus.)

4. In what parts of this painting do you see the darkest blacks? (Nostrils, part of glasses, within curls of hair, where Frank's lips meet, etc.) Where do you see white highlights? (T-shirt collar, glasses, and especially on head hair and chin hairs.)

5. What aspects of this painting are NATURALISTIC? (Facial features, hair, details of clothing.) What aspects are not naturalistic? (Color and scale.)

6. Did Chuck Close give you any clues about his friend Frank's personality? If so, what clues? What did he do to prevent us from getting too much information about Frank? (He posed him with a very bland expression; he gave us no clues about Frank's posture or body language; he enlarged the photograph so much that Frank seems larger than life.)
