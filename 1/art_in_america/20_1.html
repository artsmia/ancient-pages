Key Points

  • James van DerZee chronicled black life in Harlem in hundreds of photographs. Like many of his PORTRAITS of Harlem's socialites, religious leaders, and celebrities, Harlem Couple shows a view of Harlem during its cultural renaissance.
  • To avoid the mechanical look of most studio portraits, van DerZee personalized the poses of his subjects according to their wishes.
  • Van DerZee's use of repetition and contrast contribute to the strength of this photograph.
