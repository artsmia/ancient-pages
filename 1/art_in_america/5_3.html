Paul Revere

Many students of U.S. history know Paul Revere as a patriot of the American Revolution who made the famous midnight ride immortalized by Longfellow's poem. Few know, however, that he was also a silversmith. Silversmiths were among the most respected and best-paid artists in 18th-century America. Revere began his career as an apprentice in his father's silversmithing workshop in Boston. He later took over the business.
