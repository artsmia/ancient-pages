Art During the Depression

ABSTRACT and NONREPRESENTATIONAL art, which had gained favor in the United States during the 1910s and 1920s, held little meaning for poverty-stricken Americans during the Depression. Therefore, many artists turned to NATURALISTIC or seminaturalistic styles that the general public could easily understand and appreciate. They also began to paint subjects that reaffirmed the American values of family, home, and hard work.
