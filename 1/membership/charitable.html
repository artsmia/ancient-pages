How You Can Support The Minneapolis Institute of Arts

Did you know that more than 27,000 households in the Twin Cities support the Institute? Throughout its history, The Minneapolis Institute of Arts has benefited from the generosity of thousands of individuals and families. Today, voluntary contributions are increasingly important as we work to become more accessible and responsive to the evolving cultural and educational needs of our community. Although annual gifts continue to be the foundation on which we build, there are many other ways in which donors support the museum:

  • Gifts to Named Endowed Funds for Programs, Lectures, Art Acquisitions and Scholarships

  • Gifts of Art
  • Gifts of Cash or Securities
  • Gifts of Real Estate or Valuables
  • Gifts of Life Insurance
  • Gift of a Home or Farm
  • Gifts through Your Will

 

Taking the First Steps

Friends of The Minneapolis Institute of Arts often ask how to begin considering whether a life income or other form of planned gift is appropriate for them. The first step is easy: call our Office of Planned Giving. We'll talk with you confidentially about what kinds of arrangements might be most helpful to you and how your gift might eventually benefit the museum. We can provide you or your attorney, tax consultant or financial advisor more specific information or clarification to fit your personal needs. We can also give you information on the following topics:

  • Membership in the New Century Society

  • Suggested language for bequests to the Institute
  • Information about proposed gifts of art
  • Evaluation of real estate gifts
  • Planning suggestions for life income gifts for yourself or loved ones
  • Gift annuities
  • Donor recognition and naming opportunities
  • Suggestions about estate and will planning
  • Gift appraisals and substantiation requirements
  • Estimates of the tax advantages of proposed gifts
  • Wealth Replacement Trusts for children or grandchildren
  • Charitable Lead Trusts
  • Charitable and Estate planning newsletters and upcoming seminars
  • Updates on new tax laws
