Suggested Questions: Grades 4-6 - Teachers Key

  1. This vessel was made in the form of a fish. Does this look like a real fish? Compare to Maria Merian's print Moths, Caterpillar, and Foliage; which artist was more interested in portraying accurate detail?

  2. What do you think this object was used for? How can you tell?

  3. Is this fish ABSTRACT or NATURALISTIC? What features of the fish did the artist emphasize? What features and details are missing? Which features are shown through sculptural means in the clay itself? Which features are painted on the surface?

  4. What material is this made of? How can you tell? Where do you think the artist found the material? Have you ever made anything out of clay?

  5. Why do you think that fish might be important to the Nazca people? (Consider: food, ecology, economy, climate, geography, religion.) How are fish important to us today? (They are still an important source of food and nourishment. Fishing is a popular form of recreation. Fish are important in the ecological chain since they help maintain the balance in nature; they feed on some plants and animals and become food for others.)

  6. The Nazca people lived in river valleys near the ocean, mountains, and desert. Why do you think rainfall was so important to them? Why were the Nazca people concerned with ECOLOGY? What concerns do we have regarding ecology today? Do we worry about a shortage of water today? Volunteer might discuss issues about pollution due to industrial waste, oil spills, sewage, etc; point out that in our modern world, growing populations and industrialization have caused natural resources to become more limited than they were in ancient times.

  7. Pretend that you are an ARCHAEOLOGIST who discovered this vessel. What clues does it give you about the Nazca people? (They had the technology to fire clay; they lived near water; they were fishing people; they decorated their CERAMIC vessels.) What other areas of their culture would you explore to help you understand its meaning? (The natural environment; the burial sites; other vessels and their images.)
  8. This vessel may have been made to be included in a tomb. What other object in this unit was made for a tomb? In what ways are these objects similar? (They are both made of ceramics; both show animals respected by the artist's cultures, etc.) How are they different?
