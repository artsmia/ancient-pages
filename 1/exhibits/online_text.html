Online Exhibitions
Arts of Japan


Arts of Japan is a growing online resource featuring objects from The Minneapolis Institute of Arts' Asian collection. Current subject areas include Japanese Buddhism, Ukiyo-e woodblock prints, and Japanese scrolls and screens.
Requires QuickTime

www.artsmia.org/arts-of-asia
Restoring a Masterwork


The Restoring a Masterwork Web site provides an in-depth look at the process of restoring a 300-year-old painting from the Institute's collection. This exhibit includes a daily log of the conservators' work, an overview of the history of the painting, frequently asked questions and a in-depth technical section.
Requires QuickTime and Flash.

www.artsmia.org/restoring-a-masterwork

Modernism
The gift of Norwest Bank's Modernism collection in 1998 catapulted the Institute into the realm of leading museums of design worldwide. This program provides a wealth of information on this exciting collection and covers the six major movements represented, from Arts and Crafts through Art Nouveau, the Wiener Werkstatte, de Stijl, Bauhaus and Art Deco.

www.artsmia.org/modernism
Foot in the Door
 This online exhibition allows visitors to learn more about The Foot in the Door Show 2000 and its history, to browse through the amazingly diverse body of work, and to search for specific works of art within the show.

www.artsmia.org/foot_in_the_door
