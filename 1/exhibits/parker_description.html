Help Wanted: The Photographs of Lewis W. Hine and David L. Parker

May 22 - September 26, 1999
Harrison Photography Gallery

The role of art as social commentary and a tool for change is explored in this new exhibition featuring photographs of child labor by Lewis W. Hine (1874-1940) and David L. Parker (b. 1951).

Early in this century, Lewis W. Hine turned his lens on the social, industrial and human abuses associated with child labor. His images proved to be so persuasive that they effectively helped to secure labor and safety laws for all workers –both children and adults— in the United States.

Almost 75 years later, David L. Parker, a Minneapolis physician and artist, took up the cause of photographing child laborers around the world. The children he photographs work in a variety of environments –from Central America and North Africa to Turkey, India and Asia. His images are intended to provoke questions about how and where children should be allowed to work, what role global trade plays in the perpetuation of child labor and whether the rights of children should rise above the sovereign rights of nations.

A symposium related to photography and social concerns is being planned for September, 1999. Please call (612) 870-3131 for further information.
