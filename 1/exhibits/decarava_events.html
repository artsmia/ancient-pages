City Portraits: A Family Workshop
March 14 & 21 or March 21 & 28

In collaboration with pARTs Photographic Arts Gallery, the MIA is proud to present City Portraits, a workshop in which families learn about photographic portraits. The first session, held at pARTs gallery, focuses on how the camera works and the printing process. After learning what makes a picture a portrait, class participants have their family portraits taken with an antique 4x5 camera and then create their own portfolio of city portraits in the surrounding neighborhood. In the second session, held at the MIA, participants examine their developed portfolios, explore the special photography exhibition, Roy DeCarava: Retrospective, and learn how to interpret their own city portraits through the technique of photomontage.

Classes are held on two consecutive Sundays, first at pARTs, 711 West Lake Street (612/824-5500), and then at The Minneapolis Institute of Arts in classroom 112/113. Sections limited to 30.

Adult Class : Considering Roy DeCarava
Saturday, March 20, 1999; 10:30 a.m. - 3:30 p.m.

The artist Roy DeCarava's work will be discussed in terms of race consciousness and the historically specific conditions of African Americans in Harlem in the mid-twentieth century. Students are encourgaed to analyze the African American experience as perceived through the lens of DeCarava. Formal aspects of his aesthetic vision will be addressed through a tour of the exhibition Roy DeCarava: A Retrospective.

Family Day: Picture Perfect
Sunday, March 21, 1999, noon to 5 p.m.

Discover the work of celebrated Harlem-born photographer Roy DeCarava. Throughout the day families will view the special exhibition Roy DeCarava: A Retrospective as well as learn about the art of photography, listen to jazz music and participate in a studio art activity.
