Carol Padberg
Subject, 1997
Cibachrome

 

Padberg/Toscani

April 23—June 27, 1999
Minnesota Artists Gallery

Inter-media artist, Carol Padberg, and sculptor, Amy Toscani, explore issues related to domesticity and the artistic aspects of everyday life in this exhibition of new and recent work.

Padberg uses pattern and repetition to address the daily litany of tasks associated with parenting. Her densely layered abstractions employ a combination of sewing, collage, painting and digital photography. Particularly through her collage, Padberg explores the place between order and chaos, what she calls "the texture of everyday life." Her work references traditionally feminine tasks, such as quilt-making and domestic record-keeping, which are often undervalued by contemporary society.

Toscani is known for large, machine-like forms constructed from sheets of welded steel. She sees herself as a product of the sixties –her sculpture is reminiscent of pop culture in that she includes everyday materials such as lawn chair fabric, rubber and TV trays to fabricate whimsical, ridiculous and sometimes ominous constructions. Through her work, Toscani forges a fresh relationship between the beautiful and the mundane.
