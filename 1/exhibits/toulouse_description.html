Toulouse-Lautrec and the Performers of Paris

February 27 - September 19, 1999
19th Century Gallery

Henri de Toulouse-Lautrec (1864 - 1901) celebrated the Parisian café society that was at the height of its popularity during the turn of the century. He spent many nights visiting some of the over 27,000 cafés, dance halls and cabarets of Paris, including a nightly stop at the famous Moulin Rouge cabaret in Montmartre. As he became familiar with the performers, he began capturing their images and bold styles for his posters and lithographs. These works featured large areas of flat, bold color and captured the featured performer’s presence and spirit. Visitors can experience Toulouse-Lautrec’s vivid depiction of café life by viewing this intimate display of about 14 of his lithographs from the Institute’s permanent collection.
