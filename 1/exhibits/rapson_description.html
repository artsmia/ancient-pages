Ralph Rapson: Sixty Years of Modern Design
About the Exhibit

MIA Exhibition March 27 - July 25, 1999
Weisman Exhibition March 27 - May 22, 1999

The Minneapolis Institute of Art and the Weisman Art Museum honor Ralph Rapson, one of the leaders in modern design and architecture, with the first retrospective exhibition of his most significant projects. Rapson's designs contributed to the evolution of modern style which began in the late 1930s and gained momentum in the years following World War II. The MIA's installation focuses on Rapson's numerous residential design projects, many of which are located in the Twin Cities. The Weisman Art Museum will feature Rapson's public commissions such as the Guthrie Theatre and the urban planning project Cedar-Riverside. Combined, the two installations contain over 250 architectural elements, models, furniture, photographs and drawings, many on display for the first time.
