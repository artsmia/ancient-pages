Montmartre and the Making of Mass Culture Symposium
Friday, March 5, 1999 8:30 a.m. - 5 p.m.
Saturday, March 6, 1999 10 a.m. - 5 p.m.

What happens when bohemians, religious conservatives and anarchists come together at the end of the century? Join us for a two-day conference on the community of Montmartre in Paris in the 1890s, when popular culture and fine art first began to forge an alliance that remains with us today. Discover the role cabaret performers, visual artists, religious institutions and street life played in creating a new mass culture. The conference will provide both a historic context for questions about publicly accessible visual culture and a critical basis for examining issues surrounding this culture today.

This symposium is held in conjunction with the exhibition Henri de Toulouse- Lautrec and the Performers of Paris, on view in the 19th century Prints and Drawings gallery from February 27 - September 19, 1999. Symposium attendees will also have the pleasure of viewing a private exhibition in the Herschel V. Jones Print Study Room featuring the works of Edgar Cahine, Pablo Picasso and Théophile Steinlen.

For more information, or to register, please call the museum's Visitor and Member Center at 612/870-3131. Registration is free but pre-registration is required.
