Seeing is believing.

Every day our eyes are barraged with thousands of images. Our brain interprets these images to help us integrate what we see into our daily lives. But how often do we really LOOK at what we’re seeing?

Learning to read images, like learning to read books, is a skill that requires time and practice. In our image-rich world, the habits of careful looking can be applied beyond the walls of the art museum. Looking at art is an enjoyable way to practice essential critical thinking and communication skills.

Teachers taking The Art of Looking will

  • Practice careful looking in the galleries of The Minneapolis Institute of Arts
  • Refine their vocabulary for talking about art
  • Investigate the process of perception
  • Observe how the choices artists make impact perception
  • Explore ways to use art in the classroom to develop visual literacy and critical thinking skills in support of the Minnesota Graduation Standards
