Permanant Collection Tours

All tours are free unless otherwise indicated. Grade-level recommendations are indicated for each tour, and certain tours can be adapted to a variety of age levels, abilities, and interests. We also can customize your tour to complement specific classroom studies, and many of the tours will help your students achieve the new Minnesota Profile of Learning Preparatory and High Standards. Be sure to consult the matrices in this catalog to determine which tours will prepare your students to meet those standards.

African Art
(grades 4–12)

Explores the rich diversity of masks, textiles, and other objects from many parts of Africa. Enriches studies of African geography, cultures, and literature. Be sure to visit our newly expanded African galleries.

American Art
(grades 4–12)

Enlivens history and literature by featuring objects created by Americans of diverse backgrounds, from ancient times to the present. Includes art by America's indigenous peoples.

Art of Ancient Cultures
(grades 4–12)

Compares objects made by numerous ancient cultures, including those of Nigeria, China, Mexico, and France. Examines how such factors as environment and technology influenced art production.

Art of the Americas
(grades 1–12)

Examines a variety of objects made by the indigenous peoples of North, Central, and South America. Also considers the spiritual and philosophical significance of the works.

Art of the Ancient Mediterranean
(grades 4–12)

Transports students back in time to explore art from the Paleolithic era, Mesopotamia, Egypt, Greece, and Rome. Complements studies of world history, the humanities, or the Latin language.

Asian Art
(grades 4–12)

Explores artworks from China, Japan, India, and Southeast Asia in the museum's newly expanded Asian art galleries.

Chinese Art
(grades 4–12)

Surveys the development of Chinese art from ancient times to the present and includes bronzes, jades, ceramics, paintings, and furniture. The recently opened Chinese galleries feature hundreds of works never on view before!

City and Country
(grades 1–8)

Considers how people relate to their environment by viewing landscapes and city scenes. Enhances studies of geography, communities, and neighborhoods.

Classics and the Classical Influence
(grades 4–12)

Traces the influence of ancient Greece and Rome through the centuries. Recommended for humanities and Latin language classes.

Come to Your Senses
(grades k–3)

Investigates how artists suggest the senses of touch, taste, smell, sight, and sound in their works.

Encounter and Exchange
(grades 4–12)

Explores what happens artistically when two or more cultures meet. Chinese export ceramics in European styles, Islamic jewelry from North Africa, and the classical elements found in the Institute's original building are some of the topics that could be addressed.

European Art
(grades 6–12)

Considers art from the Middle Ages to the 18th century in its cultural context. Includes works by Rembrandt, Rubens, El Greco, Poussin, and Tintoretto.

Highlights of the Museum's Collection
(grades 1–12)

Features a selection of our best-known artworks done in a variety of materials and techniques from different periods and cultures. The tour can explore such diverse topics as What is art? What is beauty? or What can art reveal about how people lived? This tour can be customized to help students achieve certain Preparatory and High Standards.

How Was It Made?
(grades 4–12)

Examines how the materials, methods, and circumstances of production affect finished artworks. This tour can incorporate a discussion of cultural and technological advances that influenced art production.

India and Southeast Asia
(grades 6–12)

Investigates art made by the peoples of India, Tibet, Nepal, Bhutan, Vietnam, and Cambodia.

Introduction to the Museum
(grades K–2)

Helps students learn what an art museum is and how to enjoy the many different kinds of objects found in it.

Japanese Art
(grades 4–12)

Explores the development of Japanese art and culture using paintings, woodblock prints, sculptures, and ceramics.

Literature and Art
(grades 9–12)

Enhances literature studies by examining parallels in painting, sculpture, and the decorative arts. This tour is designed to complement a particular theme, period, or culture your class is studying. Request at least six weeks in advance.

Math and Art
(grades 7–12)

Considers what mathematicians and artists have in common. Students discover how artists use such mathematical principles as ratio and proportion to create works of art.

Music and Art
(grades 9–12)

Compares how a composer's use of tone, dynamics, and rhythm relates to an artist's choice of line, shape, and color. Limited to 30 students.

Mysteries in the Museum
(grades 1–5)

Helps students solve artistic mysteries and puzzles by reading clues left by artists from around the world and across time.

Mythology around the World
(grades 1–12)

Examines how people of different periods and cultures have created stories to answer fundamental questions about human experience.

19th- and 20th-Century Art
(grades 7–12)

Traces the revolutionary developments found in 19th- and 20th- century art and how they reflected social changes.

Passport
(grades 6–12)

Takes students on an art adven-ture designed to complement their foreign-language studies. Ask about tours in French, German, or Spanish. Request at least six weeks in advance.

People and Places
(grades 1–5)

Explores artists' representations of how people lived at different times and in different places.

The Period Rooms and Decorative Arts
(grades 1–12)

Takes students on a walk through history in the museum's extensive decorative arts galleries. During December the period rooms are decorated for the holidays. Enlivens a study of English, French, or American history. Plan a visit to our new Midwestern galleries!

Photography
(grades 6–12)

Examines the subjects and techniques that have interested photographers since the 19th century.

Safari through Art
(grades K–3)

Helps younger students search for real and imaginary animals in a variety of artworks.

Science and Art
(grades 1–12)

Investigates the many connections between science and art by studying diverse subjects and media. Topics could include space and time, motion, health, or the environment.

Sculpture
(grades 4–12)

Explores the range of forms, materials, and techniques artists use to create both reliefs and sculptures in-the-round.

Spirituality and Art
(grades 7–12)

Considers how religious beliefs have been expressed around the world from ancient to modern times.

Visual Elements
(grades 1–12)

Analyzes how artists use color, line, texture, and shape in countless ways to create unique works. A perfect complement to your studio-arts curriculum.

Women and Art
(grades 4–12)

Examines art created by and about women through the ages. Enriches a variety of multicultural subjects.
