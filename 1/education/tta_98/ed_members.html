Educational Services throughout Minnesota

Even if you can't make it to Minneapolis, you can still take advantage of the Institute's resources. In addition to sending materials by mail, we offer several other ways to access the museum and its collection.

Curriculum Materials On-line

A wealth of museum-related information is available on the World Wide Web, including a version of this catalog with descriptions of classroom materials and an expanding number of teaching resources. Our newest on-line curriculum unit, World Ceramics, was developed through the Integrated Arts Information Access Project (IAIA). A resource for teachers and students, the ceramics unit includes role-playing and inquiry activities for learners at all levels. To ask for a museum representative to attend an in-service and introduce our on-line curriculum materials, call (612) 870-3190 or send an E-mail to pjones@artsMIA.org. This service will be provided on a limited basis, so make your requests early. Other curriculum units available on-line include: Amazing Animals in Art, Art in America, World Mythology in Art, and Grace Hartigan's Billboard. Access our site at www.artsMIA.org.

ArtsNetMinnesota

This interactive educational resource has been created by The Minneapolis Institute of Arts, the Walker Art Center, the Frederick R. Weisman Art Museum, the Minnesota Museum of American Art, and teachers throughout Minnesota. While exploring this interactive web site, you can view images and discover interdisciplinary curriculum resources from four art museums, download lesson plans written by museum staff and teachers, and participate in an on-line curriculum exchange. This site will evolve with teachers' input, so please join the conversation! Access the site at www.ArtsNetMN.org.

ArtsConnectEd

ArtsConnectEd is a new Internet-based gateway for Minnesota's teachers, students, and parents to access the combined educational resources of The Minneapolis Institute of Arts and the Walker Art Center. ArtsConnectEd will lead the user to a database of collections and curriculum information from both museums that includes images, text archives, videos, audio, and related software. Our goal is to make arts education timely, engaging, interactive, and pertinent for both teachers and students. Teachers will be especially interested in the curriculum materials produced by the museums, and students can use the resources for self-paced learning. ArtsConnectEd can be accessed from either museum's web site or directly by visiting www.ArtsConnectEd.org. ArtsConnectEd is a partnership made possible by generous grants from MCI and the Department of Children, Families, and Learning.

ArtReach Centers

Many of our curriculum materials are available free of charge at several sites in Minnesota. You can preview and check out these materials at the McRostie Art Center, Grand Rapids, (218) 326-2697; Tweed Museum of Art, Duluth, (218) 726-8222; Owatonna Arts Center, Owatonna, (507) 451-0533; and New York Mills Cultural Center, New York Mills, (218) 385-3339. This service is made possible by a generous grant from the Blandin Foundation. Call Susan Hundman at (612) 870-3134 for more information and a brochure of available materials.
