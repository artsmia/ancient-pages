Tour Workshops

Involve your students in a tour workshop that incorporates both classroom and gallery activities. Tour workshops last about 1 1/2 hours.

Pre-workshop materials will be sent to you after you schedule a session. There is a limit of 30 students per workshop and a charge of $1 per student.

Africa
(grades 4–6)

Students explore the diversity of African geography, peoples, and art by looking at masks, textiles, and sculpture and by sketching patterns in the galleries.

Art of the Americas
(grades 1–6)

Students learn about the importance of storytelling and animals to the art and traditions of indigenous peoples from North, Central, and South America.

Egypt
(grades 4–6)

Students decipher hieroglyphics and other images on tomb objects to learn about ancient Egyptian culture and beliefs.

Writing in the Galleries

Relates art to themes your students are studying through a variety of writing exercises, including descrip-tion, comparison, poetry, dialogue, and reporting. Limited to 60 students. Request at least six weeks in advance. Not offered in April or May. There is a charge of $5 per 15 students.

CTC Plays into Art

Follow a Children's Theatre Company play with a related tour at the museum. Call (612) 870-3140 to see if a tour is available for the play your students are attending. To provide a quality experience, space is limited to 90–120 students per hour, depending on the tour.
