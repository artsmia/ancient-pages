Group Tours

Volunteer docents lead tours for 10 or more. Tours encourage students to discuss their own opinions and observations about art from around the world. A question as simple as What's going on in this picture? can elicit surprisingly different responses. Questions like Why do you think the artist chose to paint the sky yellow? or How would the sculpture look if it were clay instead of wood? invite students to think analytically and to share their responses with others.

Most tours last about one hour. Tours fill quickly, so call early. Best bets are fall and winter. (Many of our spring tours are completely booked by late fall.) All tours are free, except writing tours and workshops.

How to Schedule a Tour

Call at least four weeks in advance, Tuesday through Friday, 8:30 a.m.–5 p.m., and tell us:

  • Date and time
  • Alternate date and time
  • Number in group
  • Name of school or organization
  • Subject of tour
  • Name of teacher or group leader
  • Teacher's or leader's home telephone number
  • Telephone number and address of school or organization
  • Special requests

We'll let you know how to prepare your group and how to borrow a pre-tour video when you call to book a tour. You will receive written confirmation of your reservation three weeks before your tour date, and a docent will call you about one week prior to your tour to discuss your group's needs.

Please divide your students into groups of 10 to 15 before you arrive. One adult chaperone is required for every group during the tour. Chaperones should not bring preschool children.

Student Lunch Area

Students can eat lunches they bring in the Norwest Community Room. The space is limited to 60 students at a time and is scheduled on a first-call basis. Reservations must be made in advance.

Pre-Tour Videos

We highly recommend you show one of the following videos to help prepare your students for their museum visit. Call (612) 870-3134 for video reservations. There is a $3.50 charge for postage.

Your Trip to the Art Museum, an eight-minute video, explains what a museum is, models appropriate behavior, and excites students about their upcoming visit. Recommended for grades 1–6.

Welcome to the MIA, a seven-minute video, prepares junior high and secondary students for their museum visit. It explains what a museum is, models appropriate behavior, and previews highlights of the Institute's collection. Recommended for grades 7–12.

Students with Special Needs

One-on-one tours allow students with visual impairments to experience art through touch. Tours also can be interpreted for students with hearing impairments. Small-group tours are available for visitors using wheelchairs. Request all these tours at least four weeks in advance.
