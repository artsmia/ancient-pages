Special Exhibition Tours

Several of our special exhibition tours would be valuable in helping secondary students achieve certain standards in the new Minnesota Profile of Learning. See the tour descriptions for suggestions on how the tours can be used to help attain specific graduation standards.

To schedule a tour, call Debra DeForest or Paula Albrecht at (612) 870-3140.

For more information on other special exhibitions and events, call (612) 870-3200 or access our World Wide Web site at www.artsMIA.org.

The Mechanics of Motion: Photographs by Eadweard Muybridge and Harold E. Edgerton
Tours offered October 6, 1998– January 10, 1999

This exhibition explores the pioneering stop-action photo-graphs of Eadweard Muybridge and Harold E. Edgerton. Featured images include Muybridge's famous sequences of people and animals in motion from the 1880s and Edgerton's mid-20th century pictures of such spectacular moments as a bullet shattering a light bulb. A tour of this show could enhance studies of photography, motion, and science history.

A free Teacher Tour on photography, including a visit to this exhibition, will be given on October 8 at 4:30 p.m. Call 870-3140 to reserve your place.

Isn't S/He a Doll? Play and Ritual in African Sculpture
Tours offered November 14, 1998– January 17, 1999

Comprised of 212 sculptures from 24 countries, this exhibition examines the rich variety of forms and functions of African "dolls." This exhibit would be a great way to help secondary students attain the Diverse Perspectives standards in the People and Cultures learning area.

A free Teacher Tour of this exhibition will be given on December 10 at 4:30 p.m. Call 870-3140 to reserve your place.

Holiday Traditions in the Period Rooms
Tours offered November 27, 1998– January 3, 1999

Decorated English and American period rooms provide holiday cheer and the opportunity to learn about traditional holiday customs and their origins.

Ancient Gold: Jewelry from the Dallas Museum of Art
Tours offered December 20, 1998– March 7, 1999

This exhibition of 105 gold objects ranges from Etruscan pins and earrings to ancient Greek and Roman necklaces, bracelets, and armlets. The jewelry, from the 7th century b.c. to the 3rd century a.d., will be supplemented with works from the Institute's collection to show how the pieces were made and worn.

A free Teacher Tour of the Art of the Ancient Mediterranean, including a visit to the jewelry exhibit, will be given January 14 at 4:30 p.m. Call (612) 870-3140 to reserve your place.

Roy DeCarava: A Retrospective
Tours offered February 13– April 25, 1999

This exhibition features Roy DeCarava's photographs of African American life from 1949 to 1994. The photographs include intimate still lifes, portraits of jazz musicians, and images of daily life that subtly address the injustice of racial discrimination.

A free Teacher Tour will be given on March 11 at 4:30 p.m. Call (612) 870-3140 to reserve your place.

Ralph Rapson: Sixty Years of Modern Design
Tours offered March 27–July 25, 1999

Ralph Rapson helped introduce modern aesthetics and design to Midwestern architecture, from such features as glass curtain walls and light-capturing ceilings to organic, curving forms and shapes. This exhibition, held concurrently at both the Institute and the Frederick R. Weisman Art Museum, will include architectural drawings and models, furniture, photographs, and video installations. A tour of this exhibit could aid secondary students in attaining the Shape, Space, and Measurement standard in the Math Applications learning area.

A free Teacher Tour will be given on April 8 at 4:30 p.m. Call (612) 870-3140 to reserve your place.
