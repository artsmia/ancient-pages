Classroom Materials for Rent

Rent our materials to enhance your classroom curriculum or to complement a tour. Many of our resources can also help your students meet the new Minnesota Profile of Learning standards. Most materials can be used by K–12; however, grade-level recommendations are indicated.

Call Susan Hundman at (612) 870-3134 to reserve the following materials and for advice on using them in your classroom. Curriculum consultations and previews also are available by appointment, Tuesday–Friday, 10 a.m.–5 p.m., and the second Thursday of every month, 1–9 p.m. Materials can be picked up or mailed to you; see postage costs below.

 Free Materials

Items distributed for the National Gallery of Art, Washington, D.C. No charge for a one- or two-week rental of these materials. Overdue fine: $1 per day.

 Internet

Features on-line resources for teachers and information and activities for learners of all ages.

 Kits

Contain hands-on materials, slides, and a teacher's guide. $10–20 per two-week rental. Overdue fine: $1 per day.

 Laserdiscs

May contain both video programs and random access to still-frame "catalogs" of images. Images and details may be accessed randomly and shown in a sequence related to your choice of theme and subject.

A laserdisc player compatible with any video monitor with RCA audio and video inputs is available for $10 per two-week rental. Overdue fine for laserdisc player: $5 per day.

Laserdiscs are available for two weeks at no charge.

 Slide Sets

Contain slides plus written information. Some have audio or video cassettes. Many have suggestions for classroom activities. $5 per two-week rental, unless otherwise noted. Overdue fine: $1 per day. Postage and handling: $3.75.

 Videocassettes

Available on 1/2-inch VHS. $5 per week rental. Overdue fine: $1 per day. Postage and handling: $3.50. (cc) indicates closed captioned for the hearing impaired.

 Walker Art Center Materials

Related classroom materials can be found at the Walker Art Center. See the Walker Art Center's Educational Resources catalog or for more information, call (612) 375-7609.
