Middle Level: Grades 6-8

Middle-Level Standard: Shape, Space, and Measurement

Determine whether an artwork is two- or three-dimensional and how a two-dimensional surface can create the illusion of space.

Identify patterns and geometric shapes as elements of visual images.

Examine the concepts of space, distance, height, and perspective in relation to the untitled photograph by Jane Tuckerman. (See Amazing Animals Activities, Mathematics.)

Solve measurement problems posed in relation to Jane Tuckerman's untitled photograph. (See Amazing Animals Activities, Mathematics.)

Observe and discuss relative size and proportion of different parts of animals' bodies. (See Amazing Animals Activities, Mathematics.)
