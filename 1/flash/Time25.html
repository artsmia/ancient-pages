Rococo (1700-1770s)

The rococo began in France as a direct contradiction to the heavy and dramatic Baroque. The religious and classical subjects of past movements were rejected in favor of frivolous, sensual, and clever subjects that better reflected the free and easy lifestyles of the French aristocracy. While bright and intense colors characterized the Baroque, the elegant pastels and pinks of the rococo carried an air of playfulness and festivity, which gives the paintings a light and fluffy feeling.
