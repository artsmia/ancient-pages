Castiglione in Genoa and Rome (1640s)

The time Castiglione spent in Genoa between 1639 and 1646 is considered his principal mature period. It is around this time that he paints An Allegory of Vanity, which now hangs in the Nelson-Atkins Museum of Fine Art in Kansas City. Although a non-religious scene, this early work already displays the sense of drama and color exhibited in The Immaculate Conception.
Giovanni Benedetto Castiglione
Allegory of Vanity
1647-1649
Oil on Canvas
39 x 56-11/16 in.
