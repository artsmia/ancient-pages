Realism and Impressionism (1840-1890s)

Realism is an art movement that emphasized subject matter other than the traditional themes of history, mythology and religion. Realist art portrays unidealized scenes of modern life. A realist's goal is to create accurate, objective images of the common man living in the modern world.

The Impressionist movement came to life in the 1870s. Impressionists wanted to record nature by presenting a general impression of a scene from life. The works are objective and scientific in spirit; they attempt to simulate the actual refraction of light. Small brushstrokes of unmixed primary colors laid against one another let the viewer's eye mix the colors rather than premixing them on the palette.
