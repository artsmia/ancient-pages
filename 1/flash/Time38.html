First public air-conditioning installed (1922)

Climate control in museums is very important to the preservation of works of art to help prevent exposure of delicate and often quite old artifacts to extremes in temperature. Museums today control the temperature and humidity of their galleries. The ideal environment for this Castiglione painting is between 68 and 70 degrees F. with 48 percent to 50 percent humidity.

Air-conditioning was invented in the early part of the 20th century by Willis Carter, of Buffalo, New York. However, it wasn't until 1922 that the first air-conditioner was installed in Graumann's Metropolitan Theatre in Los Angeles.
