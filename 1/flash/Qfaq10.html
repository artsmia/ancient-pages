10. All art materials have pros and cons. Oil paint is a beautiful medium, allowing the painter to create effects such as glazing or working wet-into-wet. Oil paint, however, is not appropriate for inpainting as it darkens with age and becomes difficult to remove from an original oil paint layer. We are using dry pigments ground in a stable synthetic medium for inpainting. The synthetic medium does not darken or discolor with age, but it demands a good deal of skill to make a synthetic look like a beautiful, aged oil paint. Most important, the synthetic medium paint can be very safely removed from the painting.
