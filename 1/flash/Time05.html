Tintoretto (Jacopo Robusti)
The Raising of Lazarus
About 1558-1559
Oil on canvas
H.70-7/8 x W.108-1/4 in.
The Centennial Fund

The raising of Lazarus from the dead was one of the miracles performed by Christ. Tintoretto excelled at large, crowded paintings of biblical events, infused with spiritual intensity and meant to appeal to the viewer's emotions. Exploiting Mannerist devices such as elongated forms, exaggerated perspective, startling juxtapositions of vivid colors, and theatrical lighting, he achieved scenes of stirring drama. Tintoretto laid out his compositions quickly, employing a Venetian technique of vigorous, broken brushwork called alla prima. This involved applying one layer of paint over another, before the first layer dried, so that mixing of the colors took place directly on the canvas. At the upper right, Tintoretto may have included his self-portrait as the man seen from behind in conversation with the painter Titian.
