First commercial art materials (Late 1700s)

Traditionally, painters worked within the guild system. This system supported apprentices, who would learn a particular craft by working with a mentor or master. With the commercial availability of packaged paints and other artists' materials, painting production begins to change. Artists no longer needed to mix their paints or construct their own supports and frames, tasks performed previously by apprentices. Thus, the guild system of painting began to break down.
