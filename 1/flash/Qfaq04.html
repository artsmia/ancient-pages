4. When cleaning a painting, it is very important to know the difference between an original paint layer and overpaint added to repair or disguise damage. Very often, paintings were restored using an oil paint medium. The newer oil paint may have matched the original layers for a period of time, but oil paint darkens slightly with age, and soon, the restored area is easily detected. If a painting is restored using stable, non-discoloring synthetic resin paint, the inpainting will not darken.However, the synthetic can be safely removed from an original paint layer with solvents that will not harm the painting.

An experienced paintings conservator has tools at their disposal to detect restoration. Both oil and synthetic resin overpaint or inpainting will fluoresce a different color under an ultraviolet light source. Infrared reflectography is also helpful. Most essential is the experienced eye of the paintings conservator.
