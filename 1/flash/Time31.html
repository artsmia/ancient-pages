Photography emerges (1839)

From the collection

William Henry Fox Talbot
Cloisters of Lacock Abbey
About 1844
Cabtype
H.6-1/2 x W.8-1/16 in.
The John R. Van Derlip Fund

The invention of photography had a great impact on painters. Not only did it change the way that painters looked at composition, it also changed the artists' subject choices. A realistic representation of the world no longer had to be represented in paint, so painters could experiment with other subjects and concepts.
