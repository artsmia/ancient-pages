Titian (Tiziano Vecellio)
The Temptation of Christ
About 1530
Oil on panel
overall H.36-1/8 x W.28-7/16 in.
The William Hood Dunwoody Fund

The Baroque artist Titian was well known for his compositions and energetic brushstrokes. The Temptation of Christ shows the keen sense of color used by the Venetian Baroque artists.

Castiglione was greatly influenced by the works of Titian, and may have seen woodblock prints of Titian's famous Assumption of 1518, while he was painting The Immaculate Conception.
