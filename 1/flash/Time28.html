The "modern museum" (1765)

In 1765 philosopher Denis Diderot outlined a plan for a National French Museum that resonates today with basic museum goals. He said museums should preserve and interpret the tangible evidence of humankind for the public. This concept propels museums even today.

The Enlightenment, an 18th-century philosophy based on individualism, science and reason, brought about many new theories and concepts about museums. Previously museums had been primarily collections of curiosity items, not the keepers of a nation's treasures. Many museums opened in the later 18th century, including the Louvre (1793), and The British Museum (1759).

Charles Wilson Peale opened the first American museum in Philadelphia in 1786. His museum, like many others of this time, was filled with paintings (many by himself) and artifacts of science and nature, including the skeleton of a woolly mammoth.
