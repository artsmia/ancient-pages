Neoclassicism and Romanticism (1750-1850)

The discovery of Pompeii and Herculanium sparked a renewed interest in the classical art of Rome and Greece. The neoclassical art of the 18th century reveals an effort to recapture the order, clarity and reason of Greek and Roman art.

Although no one style can be defined as "romantic art," subject matter can be more easily identified as "romantic." Characteristics of romantic art include a deepened appreciation of the beauty of nature, emphasis on emotion, and images of the hero or heroine sacrificing for the greater good. Human passions and fears of humans are depicted, often through scenes of the exotic and the occult.
