5. Surely, most paintings conservators have worked on scores of different styles of paintings. Conserving a painting is not repainting a work of art; rather, losses, abrasion, and damage are very carefully inpainted. A stable, non-yellowing or non-discoloring synthetic medium is used in thin, transparent layers and applied only to the areas of damage. The surrounding original paint color and tonality is used to guide the conservator during the inpainting process.
