Post-Impressionism, Symbolism and Art Nouveau (1880-1910)

Post-Impressionists were a diverse group of artists. They wanted to move away from the classical aesthetic promoted by the academies of art. Leaders such as Cézanne and Mates rejected the objectivity of the Impressionists, instead using color, line and form to interpret the world in their own way.

Instead of using art to illustrate events the Symbolists strove Symbolist art are often metaphorical. Symbolists believed color could be expressive of certain emotions. Art Nouveau was an international movement primarily used as design ornament.

Art Nouveau can be recognized by its sinuous and asymmetrical lines based on plant formations.
