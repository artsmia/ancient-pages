Euclidean Promenades
(Les promenades d’Euclide) 1955
oil on canvas

Painter René Magritte and photographer Stephanie Torbert share an interest in what Torbert calls “the surrealism of everyday life.” Both of these works of art ask questions about what’s inside and what’s outside, what’s real and what’s not, in situations any of us could encounter any day. “Magritte was an influence on me and one of the first exhibitions I saw that I was really struck by was a big Magritte show at the Walker Art Center in Minneapolis,” recalls Torbert. “I just loved it.”

The play between real life and image in both of these works is what causes a double-take. It wouldn’t work as well if these objects were in some exotic location or the objects themselves were unusual. This fantastic ordinariness was what Surrealists loved to represent in their work, and what Stephanie Torbert describes in her photograph.
