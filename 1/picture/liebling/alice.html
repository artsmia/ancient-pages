Jerome Liebling sent a copy of this photograph to Fredrick Longen’s daughter, Alice Saleck. She wrote to Liebling:

You sure did a wonderful job of capturing him just like we remember him when we were growing up. He was a wonderful father. We miss him so much. If ever we needed advice or help. He was always there. I can’t find words that could tell you how much you have done for us by your picture of our PA PA.

It was such a beautiful day today. My husband and I met some of the rest of the family, and we went to the Minn. Institute of Arts to see you collection. It was fabulous. You captured so many people of all kinds. In a time when things were hard. There were a lot people through out the institute. We were standing at the picture of our PA PA and the attendant came over to us. She ask us if we knew the man in the picture, because she heard us talking. We said yes. It was our PA PA. Now after all these years he is able to travel and see the world. Thanks to you.

Liebling was so proud of the letter he included it in his book, The Minnesota Photographs
