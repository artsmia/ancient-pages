Traditionally, “contrast” in photographs is defined as the difference between lights and darks in a picture. High contrast photos have bright whites and deep rich blacks. Low contrast photos lack the intense range of differences, and can appear “flat” and washed out. Good photos make the most of contrast.

Berenice Abbott
5th Avenue Houses

Variations in texture and pattern also add interest to a photograph.

Jerome Liebling
Woman, Red Lake, Minnesota 1953

Comparisons within the photograph can also add its meaning. In Eve Fuka’s photograph Baroque Street , one figure is a large, looming statue, old and dark, while the other figure is a small, swirling girl dressed in white.

Eva Fuka
