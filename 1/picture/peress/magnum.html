Gilles Peress is a member of Magnum, an agency formed by photographers to help them control the way their work appears. Members of Magnum are represented by the agency when anyone buys their photos or photo essays for the media. Magazines and newspapers are the most frequent clients. Magnum also accepts assignments from the media, to cover newsworthy events around the world. Belonging to Magnum is different from working as a freelance photojournalist or as a staff photographer for a newspaper or magazine.

Magnum photographers have a say in how their pictures appear, what publications they will appear in, and the captions that go with the pictures. It’s part of the deal when publications buy photos from Magnum.

Photographers retain the rights to their own photos when they belong to Magnum. When photographers work on staff at magazines or newspapers, or when these publications buy photos from freelance photographers, the publication often requires that the photographer turn over all rights to images. This means that the publication can resell the photo, without paying extra fees to the photographer.

Because Magnum photographers retain the rights to their photos they benefit financially whenever their work is sold, and re-sold by Magnum. This allows Magnum photographers like Gilles Peress the financial freedom to pursue stories in Bosnia or Rwanda without receiving the assignment directly or selling the photos ahead of time. This arrangement also allowed Peress to go where other photographers could not go in Iran, because he was working independently, not withAmerican news organizations or publications. In Iran, all American correspondents were confined to the area just outside the American Embassy in Teheran.

Can anyone be a member of Magnum? Nope; you have to be invited!
