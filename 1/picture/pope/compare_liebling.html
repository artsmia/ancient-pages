Both of these photographs attract our attention because of the expressions and gestures of their young subjects. Both children appear to be confronting things children shouldn’t have to think about. But there is more to these portraits than the inner worlds of two children pausing before cameras. Consider their reactions to the photographer. Every photograph is a document of the relationship between the subject in the photo and the photographer. It is possible these children’s expressions are a response to a stranger with a camera. Could this be a case where photographs hide as much as they show?
