35 mm - The plane where the film rests in a 35mm camera tilts when the whole camera tilts (see the dotted line on the camera). The camera has to tilt to include the whole building in the frame, but top of the building is farther away from the film plane. The resulting distortion makes the building appear to be falling backward in the finished picture.

View Camera - When not adjusted using built in swings and tilts, a view camera works just like a 35 mm camera - the film plane tilts when the whole camera is tilted to include all of the building in the frame.

View Camera Correction - The lens of the view camera can be adjusted to allow for angles necessary to include the whole building in the frame, and the film plane can be adjusted so that it is parallel to the building and the camera lens, eliminating distortion.
