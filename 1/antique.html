Decorative Arts Council: 17th Annual Antiques Show and Sale

October 20 - 22, noon - 9 p.m. Friday; noon - 5 p.m. Saturday and Sunday
International Market Square

Forty - one dealers will be exhibiting and selling American, European, Asian, and African antiques from the 18th, 19th, and 20th centuries. This year's theme is English and American Silver.
Daily admission is $10; includes show admission, lectures, and dealer talks.

For further information and reservations call the Antiques Show Office 612-870-3039

 

Related Events

Antiques Show and Sale Preview Party
Thursday, October 19, 6 - 9 p.m.
International Market Square
$85 fee includes the party and one - day admission to the show.

 

A Tolerable Advantageous Business: The Varied Career of Paul Revere
Friday, October 20, 11 a.m.
International Market Square

Lecturer: Janine E. Skerry Curator of Ceramics and Glass at The Colonial Williamsburg Foundation, Skerry will discuss the range of silver produced in the Revere shop and will consider Revere's additional achievements as an engraver, founder, and industrialist, and his transformation from artisan to entrepreneur.
Sponsored by the Decorative Arts Council.
$10 includes lecture and admission to the show.

 

Antiques Show Beginning Collectors' Night
Friday, October 20, 5 - 9 p.m.
International Market Square

Houston - based interior designer William W. Stubbs will present "Living with Antiques: Furnishing Interiors Today." Stubbs will discuss the use of antiques with reproductions in modern and renovated spaces.
The $24 fee includes supper, lecture, admission to the show, and participation in a $1,000 drawing.

 

All that Glitters: Silver Fakes and Forgeries
Saturday, October 21, 11 a.m.
International Market Square

Lecturer: Edgar Moreno Moreno, Conservation Fellow at Colonial Williamsburg, will explore the appreciation of silver, focusing primarily on the 18th and 19th centuries. He will discuss the design, construction, condition, and historical context of English and American wares. Special emphasis will be given to characteristics that challenge both collectors and curators.
Sponsored by the Decorative Arts Council.
$10 includes lecture and admission to the show.
