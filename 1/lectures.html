Lectures
Lectures at the Institute are offered throughout the year on a range of art-related topics ranging from the permanent collection to special presentations on visiting exhibitions and discussions of architecture and collecting. Lectures are free, unless otherwise noted.

 

Up Close and Personal
Thursday, November 9, 11 a.m.
Pillsbury Auditorium

Shirley Blanc Romaine will speak about her life as an actress. Coffee precedes lecture in the Regis Fountain court. Sponsored by the Friends of the Institute. Call (612) 870-3045.

The Prairie School and John Howe
Thursday, November 9
6 P.M. tour, 7 P.M. reception, Fountain Court

Institute curator Jennifer Komar Olivarez and research assistant Cori Wegener will give an insider’s tour of the newly installed Ulrich Architecture and Design Gallery, which features the museum’s Prairie School collection. In addition, tours of the special exhibition "John Howe in Minnesota: The Prairie School Legacy of Frank Lloyd Wright" will be led by guest curator Jane Hession and Christopher Monkhouse, James Ford Bell Curator of Decorative Arts, Sculpture & Architecture.

Charles Rennie Mackintosh and the Glasgow School: Interiors and Place
Thursday, November 16
5:30 P.M. reception; 6 P.M. lecture,
Wells Fargo Community Room

A native of Glasgow, Scotland, Lindsay Shen is director of The Goldstein: A Museum of Design at the University of Minnesota. She will discuss the art of Charles Rennie Mackintosh (1868–1928) and his contemporaries, their Art Nouveau designs, and the Scottish context of their work. Textile Council members free; nonmembers $10.

Decorative Arts Council Annual Holiday Event
James Ford Bell: The Passionate Collector as Generous Benefactor
Thursday, December 7, 6 P.M.
Free and open to the public.

Lecturer: Christopher Monkhouse
Christopher Monkhouse, the Institute’s James Ford Bell Curator, will discuss the legacy of Bell, a great benefactor to the department. Monkhouse will talk about Bell’s English and American silver, and the furnishings in the Charleston Period Room.

For information, call Nicole Gibbs at (612) 870-3101.

 

Paintings Council
The Art of the Exhibition
Thursday, December 7
Wells Fargo Room

Paintings Curator Patrick Noon and Assistant Curator Sue Canterbury will describe how temporary loan exhibitions are organized. Discussions will revolve around the upcoming "Degas in America" and "The American Sublime" exhibitions.
